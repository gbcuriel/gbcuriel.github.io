Website Evaluations

Microsoft

Microsoft's website is a clean and professional portal for their extensive range of software, hardware, and cloud services, the design is modern and user friendly, catering to both individual consumers and large corporations by showcasing products like Windows, Office, and Azure in a highly organized, grid based layout.

  • Contrast - The site uses strong contrast with bold headlines and vibrant product images against a clean background, effectively guiding user attention. This creates a clear visual hierarchy for important information.
  • Repetition - Repetition is used consistently through the site's typography, color scheme, and user interface elements. This reinforces the Microsoft brand and makes the site intuitive to navigate.
  • Alignment - The website demonstrates excellent alignment, adhering to a strict grid structure that gives the layout a sense of order. This makes the content easy to scan and read.
  • Proximity - Related items, such as product features and specifications, are grouped together logically. This helps users easily find and compare information.

Yale Art School

The Yale School of Art website is an unconventional and experimental platform that functions as a collaborative wiki for the school's community. It's design is intentionally chaotic and user generated, reflecting an avant garde artistic ethos rather than traditional web design principles, leading to a visually unpredictable experience.

  • Contrast - The site often has poor contrast, with text placed on busy, animated backgrounds or using colors that make it difficult to read. This significantly hinders the accessibility of the information.
  • Repetition - There is a deliberate lack of repetition in the website's design. This results in an inconsistent and disorienting experience for users.
  • Alignment - The site disregards standard alignment principles, resulting in a cluttered and disorganized appearance. The layout lacks a clear visual structure, making it difficult to follow.
  • Proximity - Related information is not always grouped together logically. This contributes to the site's confusing navigation and makes it hard to find specific content.
